MCS in Brief:
MCS Data Labs, a Berlin-based Small and Medium-sized Enterprise (SME) founded in 2013, stands at the forefront of technological innovation, extending far beyond the realm of smart wearable sensor devices. While the company has established a strong foothold in the development of advanced wearable technology, its expertise is not confined to this area alone. MCS Data Labs excels in creating a holistic ecosystem encompassing robust backend infrastructures and sophisticated mobile applications, integral to the functionality and effectiveness of their wearables.

MCS Data Labs is not just about wearable technology

As a research-focused entity, MCS specializes in a range of solutions pivotal to the Internet of Things (IoT), Artificial Intelligence (AI), and Big Data domains. Their expertise is not limited to the digital health sector, as they also venture into areas like cybersecurity and smart cities, showcasing their versatile technological prowess. The company’s capabilities in handling streaming data and real-time processing of big data are central to their innovative approach. This approach is not just about the wearables themselves but encompasses the entire data collection and analysis platform. Their proprietary wearable sensor devices and software applications work in tandem, monitoring health conditions and integrating environmental and motion data to establish a comprehensive AI-knowledge base and generate novel research hypotheses. MCS’s Department of Digital Health boasts a team of specialists from various fields, including general internal medicine, endocrinology, diabetes, laboratory investigations, and knowledge engineering. This diverse expertise reinforces their ability to develop not just wearables but also complete ecosystems including clinical decision support systems, cancer treatment methodologies, knowledge engineering, and domain modeling in healthcare.

In essence, MCS Data Labs is not just about wearable technology; it represents a comprehensive technological ecosystem, integrating advanced wearables, sophisticated backend systems, and user-centric mobile applications. This holistic approach positions them as a key player in driving research and development, leveraging AI, Big Data, and wearable technology to revolutionize healthcare outcomes and expand the frontiers of medical knowledge.
